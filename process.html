Process

Our engineering project focused on building a cross-disciplinary vending machine that addresses the lack of healthy snack access on campus, especially during off-hours.

The process began with design brainstorming and team role assignments, followed by ordering essential components like servo motors, polycarbonate sheets, and Arduino boards. We constructed a durable frame with modular polycarbonate panels, integrated a spiral spring dispensing system powered by high-torque servos, and assembled the internal circuitry.

We built a membrane keypad interface for user selection and connected it to an Arduino Mega 2560, which controls servo motion, sensors, and LED feedback. An IR sensor ensures successful item dispensing, and a toggle switch allows for convenient reloading.

The project involved extensive software development, implementing real-time keypad input, automatic retry logic, and inventory tracking with LED alerts. We documented our findings, created a demo video, and launched a portfolio website to showcase our progress.
